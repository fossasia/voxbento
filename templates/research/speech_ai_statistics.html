    The Impact of Low Latency in Live Translation

    Latency is the critical metric in simultaneous interpretation. When attendees listen to translated audio while watching a live speaker, visual-audio desynchronization creates cognitive dissonance.

    Latency Range Protocol Attendee Experience Impact
    Under 500ms WebRTC / WHIP / WHEP Indistinguishable from real-time. High engagement.
    1.5s - 3s RTMP / SRT Noticeable delay. Acceptable for non-visual audio, poor for live video.
    5s - 15s HLS / DASH Severe disconnect. Often causes attendees to abandon the feed.

    Speech-to-Text Accuracy Targets (WER)

    Word Error Rate (WER) measures the percentage of words incorrectly transcribed. In conference settings, technical jargon and speaker accents heavily influence WER.

    For more details on specific model performance, see our STT Provider Comparison methodology.

    Methodology for Measuring Adoption

    To accurately assess the impact of Speech AI in your events, we recommend tracking the following metrics:

    1. Session Duration vs Engagement: Do attendees using AI-translated feeds stay in the session as long as native speakers?
    2. Transcript Correction Rate: When providing post-event VODs, what percentage of the AI transcript requires manual editing?
    3. Interpreter Handoff Frequency: Does providing real-time transcripts to interpreters alter the frequency of their 15-minute handoff cycles?
