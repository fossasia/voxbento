Interpretation at live events rarely runs on a single interpreter for the entire duration. Voxbento is built for real-world shifts: you can replace the active publisher with a backup interpreter in roughly one to two seconds, and listeners never lose their connection or need to refresh. This page explains how handoff works under the hood, who can trigger one, and the exact steps for both the outgoing and incoming interpreter.

## How Handoff Works

When a room_coordinator or active interpreter triggers a handoff, Voxbento immediately updates the active interpreter and broadcasts the new roster to all participants in the booth. On the media side, the moment the incoming interpreter clicks **UNMUTE**, the media relay switches the source automatically. Listeners receive the new stream within approximately one to two seconds. No reconnection, no page refresh, no audible silence gap beyond the natural transition.

## Who Can Trigger a Handoff

### Coordinators

    Anyone with the `room_coordinator` role can set any interpreter in the roster as the active publisher at any time. This is the primary handoff mechanism during structured shifts.
  
  ### Active Interpreter

    The currently active interpreter can voluntarily hand off to a backup interpreter in the roster, even without a room_coordinator present.
  
  ### Event Owners

    Event admins have all room_coordinator permissions and can manage handoffs across every booth in their event.
  
  ### Super Admins

    Super admins have global handoff rights across every event and booth in the Voxbento instance.
  

## Steps to Hand Off (Coordinator Perspective)

As a coordinator, you initiate the handoff from the participant roster panel inside the booth.

### Locate the incoming interpreter in the roster

Open the **Participant Roster** panel. You'll see all connected participants with their role labels. Find the backup interpreter you want to promote — they should already be joined and in an idle state.
  ### Click Set Active

Click **Set Active** next to the interpreter's name. Voxbento immediately designates them as the active interpreter and broadcasts the updated roster to everyone in the booth.
  ### Old stream is replaced

Within approximately one to two seconds, the media relay switches to the new publisher and stops forwarding the old stream. The outgoing interpreter sees their Audio Status change to **Idle**.
  ### New interpreter begins speaking

Signal the incoming interpreter (via chat or a pre-agreed cue) to click **UNMUTE** and begin. Listeners hear the transition as a brief natural pause.

## Steps for the Incoming Interpreter

Smooth handoffs depend on the incoming interpreter being ready before the room_coordinator clicks **Set Active**.

### Join the booth before your shift starts

Open the booth URL and join as a participant well ahead of your scheduled slot. Stay muted — do not click **UNMUTE** yet. This ensures you're visible in the coordinator's roster.
  ### Watch for your Go Live button to activate

When the room_coordinator designates you as the active interpreter, the top-left indicator will change to **STANDBY** (or **ON AIR** if you are unmuted).
  ### Click Go Live immediately

Click **UNMUTE** as soon as you are ready. Every second of delay is a second of silence for listeners. Speak your first word within one to two seconds of clicking.

:::tip
Agree on a hand signal or chat message before the event to coordinate smooth handoffs. A simple "ready in 30 seconds" in the booth chat gives the outgoing interpreter a clear countdown and prevents premature transitions.
:::

## Using the Chat Panel for Coordination

All participants in a booth — interpreters, coordinators, and any joined listeners with appropriate roles — can send text messages using the **Chat** panel. The chat is separate from the audio stream and is never heard by listeners.

<details>
<summary>Suggested chat conventions for handoffs</summary>

Consistent shorthand makes real-time coordination faster under pressure. Consider agreeing on these phrases before your event:

  | Message | Meaning |
  |---|---|
  | `ready` | Incoming interpreter is joined and ready to go live |
  | `go` | Room Coordinator confirms: click UNMUTE now |
  | `hold` | Do not go live yet — room_coordinator is resolving an issue |
  | `back` | Outgoing interpreter is going offline (voluntary handoff) |
  | `check` | Request a mic level check from the incoming interpreter |

</details>

## If There Is No Room Coordinator in the Booth

:::info
If the room_coordinator role is not filled, the currently active interpreter can designate a new active interpreter from the roster. Any participant with the `interpreter` role who is currently set as active has permission to use the **Set Active** control — they do not need a room_coordinator to be present.
:::

In this case, the outgoing interpreter clicks **Set Active** next to the incoming interpreter's name in the roster, then clicks **MUTE** after the new stream is confirmed live. The media relay switches sources within approximately two seconds, so listeners hear no gap provided the incoming interpreter clicks **UNMUTE** promptly.
