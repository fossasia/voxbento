Going live in Voxbento means your microphone audio is published via WebRTC to the media relay, where listeners receive it in near real-time. The process is designed to be fast — from clicking **Go Live** to listeners hearing you takes only a fraction of a second. This page walks you through the preflight check, the controls available while you're on air, and how to hand back gracefully when your shift ends.

## Prerequisites

Before you attempt to go live, make sure the following are in place:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Joined the Booth" icon="door-open">
    You have opened the booth via an invite link or your registered account dashboard. See [Joining a Booth](/interpreter/joining-a-booth) if you haven't done this yet.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Mic Permission Granted" icon="microphone">
    Your browser has been given permission to access your microphone and the VU meter responds when you speak.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Interpreter or Coordinator Role" icon="shield-check">
    Your session token or account assignment grants you the `interpreter` or `coordinator` role. Listeners cannot go live.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Preflight Check

When you first open the booth, Voxbento automatically runs a preflight check before you can go live.

<Accordion title="What the preflight check verifies">
  Voxbento automatically confirms two things before you can go live:

  1. **Mic accessibility** — your browser can open your selected audio input device.
  2. **Media relay reachability** — the streaming endpoint for your language channel is reachable from your browser.

  If either check fails, the Audio Status panel shows an **Error** badge with a short description. Resolve the issue (check your mic, your network connection, or ask your admin to verify the media relay is running) before proceeding.
</Accordion>

## Steps to Go Live

<Steps>
  <Step title="Unmute your microphone">
    Check the mic toggle in the toolbar. If it shows a muted icon, click it to unmute. Watch the VU meter — you should see the bars respond as you speak.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Go Live">
    Click the **Go Live** button. Voxbento establishes a WebRTC connection to the media relay in the background. This takes only a moment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Streaming status">
    The Audio Status panel changes to **Streaming**. This confirms the media relay is receiving your audio and listeners can hear you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Begin your interpretation">
    Start speaking. The floor audio from the Jitsi iframe plays in your headset so you can follow the session. Listeners hear only your mic — not the floor audio.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Audio Controls While Live

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Mic Mute / Unmute" icon="microphone-slash">
    Toggle your mic on or off at any time without stopping the stream. Use this for brief pauses — coughing, notes, transitions — without going fully offline.
  </Card>

  <Card title="VU Meter" icon="chart-bar">
    A volume unit meter shows your current microphone level in real time. Keep levels out of the red to avoid clipping.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Floor Audio Volume" icon="volume-high">
    Adjust the volume of the Jitsi floor monitor in your headset independently. Turning this down helps reduce cognitive load during dense speech segments.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Listeners Hear

Understanding the audio routing helps you interpret with confidence:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="What listeners receive">
    Listeners connect to Voxbento via a real-time audio stream. They receive your microphone audio directly from the media relay. The latency from your voice to their ears is typically under one second on a well-configured network.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="What you hear">
    You hear the Jitsi floor session audio in your headset — the speakers, panellists, and any floor audio from the event. This feed is local only and is never forwarded to listeners.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Why floor audio is kept separate">
    Routing the Jitsi floor audio to listeners would create a feedback loop and result in double-audio (both the raw event stream and your interpretation). Voxbento isolates the two intentionally. Your mic is the only source listeners receive.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  The Jitsi floor monitor audio plays in your headset only. Listeners hear your mic, not the floor audio. This is by design to prevent feedback loops.
</Note>

## Stopping the Stream

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Go Offline">
    Click the **Go Offline** button in the toolbar. Voxbento closes your WebRTC connection and the media relay stops receiving your audio.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Listeners stay connected">
    Listener connections remain open. They will hear silence until a backup interpreter goes live or a handoff is triggered. No reconnection is required on the listener side.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Booth returns to idle">
    The Audio Status panel returns to **Idle**. You remain in the booth as a participant and can go live again at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not refresh the page while live — this will briefly interrupt the stream. If you need to recover, click **Go Live** again after the page reloads. Coordinators can reassign the active interpreter if an unexpected disconnection occurs.
</Warning>
