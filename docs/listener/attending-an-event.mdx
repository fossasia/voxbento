Voxbento lets you listen to live interpretation in your preferred language directly from a browser tab — no app download, no account sign-up, and no special hardware. All you need is the link your event organizer shared with you (or the public event URL) and a modern browser. Audio is delivered over WebRTC for near-instant playback, with an automatic fallback to HLS if your network or browser needs it.

## Accessing the Listener Page

Every Voxbento event has a dedicated listener page at `/listener/{event_slug}`. For example, the listener page for an event with the slug `pycon2026` would be:

```
https://voxbento.com/listener/pycon2026
```

### Using an Invite Link

If your organizer sent you a personal invite link, it follows the format `/join/{token}`. Clicking that link validates your token and drops you directly onto the listener page, so you never need to type in a URL manually.

:::info
Your invite token is single-use or time-limited depending on how your organizer configured it. If your link has expired, contact the event organizer for a new one.
:::

## How Audio Delivery Works

Voxbento uses **WebRTC (WHEP)** as the primary audio delivery method, achieving under one second of end-to-end latency. If WebRTC is unavailable in your environment — for example, due to a restrictive corporate firewall — Voxbento automatically falls back to an **HLS stream** with roughly three to six seconds of delay. You do not need to choose between protocols; the switch happens transparently.

:::tip
For the lowest possible latency, use a Chromium-based browser (Chrome, Edge, Brave) or Firefox on a device connected to Wi-Fi or a wired network.
:::

### Browser Autoplay and the "Click to Play" Prompt

Browsers enforce an autoplay policy that prevents audio from starting without a user gesture. If you land on the listener page and audio does not begin immediately, you will see a **Play** button. Clicking it once is enough — this is a standard browser behaviour, not a Voxbento error.

### Automatic Reconnection

If the stream briefly drops — for example, during an interpreter handoff between shifts — Voxbento reconnects automatically within a few seconds. You will see the status badge change to **Connecting…** and then return to **Live (WebRTC)** without any action from you.

## Step-by-Step: Joining a Live Event

### Get your listener link from the event organizer

Ask your organizer for either the public event URL (`/listener/{event_slug}`) or a personal invite link (`/join/{token}`). Both take you to the same listener page.
  ### Open the link in your browser

Paste or click the link in any modern browser — Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. No extension or plugin is needed.
  ### Pick your language on the listener page

The listener page shows all available language booths for this event. Click the language you want to hear to connect to that booth's audio stream.
  ### Click Play if prompted

If your browser's autoplay policy prevents audio from starting automatically, a **Play** button appears. Click it once to begin playback.
  ### Hear live interpretation

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Why is there a short delay between the speaker and what I hear?</summary>

A small amount of latency is unavoidable in any live-streaming system. When Voxbento uses WebRTC (WHEP), the delay is typically under one second. If your browser falls back to HLS, you will notice a delay of around three to six seconds. This is expected behaviour — it does not indicate a problem with your connection or the event.

</details>
  <details>
<summary>Why can I hear static or silence instead of interpretation?</summary>

The interpreter may be between shifts, preparing to go live, or there may be a momentary gap during a handoff. Try waiting a few seconds and refreshing the page if silence persists. If the issue continues, contact the event organizer to confirm that a booth is actively staffed.

</details>
  <details>
<summary>Can I listen on my phone or tablet?</summary>

Yes. Voxbento works in mobile browsers without any app download. Use **Safari** on iOS (iPhone and iPad) or **Chrome** on Android for the best experience. Make sure your device volume is turned up and that your browser has not muted the tab.

</details>
  <details>
<summary>Do I need to create a Voxbento account?</summary>

No. Listener access does not require an account. You only need your invite link or the public event URL. Account creation is reserved for interpreters and event organizers.

</details>
